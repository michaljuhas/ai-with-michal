# How AI is changing recruiting

Recruiting is shifting from a role centered on manual research and repetitive execution to one centered on judgment, system design, and leverage.

The biggest change is not that AI can do a few tasks faster. The real change is that strong recruiters can now build repeatable systems for work that used to happen one candidate, one search, and one message at a time.

## What is changing in practice

- Sourcing is moving from manual profile review to AI-assisted market research and list building.
- Screening is moving from reading everything line by line to faster pattern recognition and summarization.
- Reporting is moving from end-of-week manual updates to reusable workflows and drafts.
- Preparation is moving from "start from scratch" to using saved prompts, structured context, and repeatable systems.

## Why this matters now

If one recruiter can operate with more speed, more context, and better documentation, the baseline for good performance changes for everyone else too.

That means AI is no longer just a nice-to-have skill. It becomes part of how teams evaluate output, adaptability, and future potential.

## A better way to think about AI

Treat AI less like a chatbot you occasionally ask for help, and more like:

- a research assistant for new markets and roles
- a drafting partner for recurring communication
- a thinking partner for structuring searches and workflows
- a system layer that helps you do consistent work at higher speed

## Reflection prompt

Before the workshop, write down three recruiting tasks you still do fully manually. Those are usually the best starting points for practical AI adoption.
