# Examples of what other recruiters do

One of the fastest ways to get practical with AI is to stop thinking in generic use cases and start looking at concrete recruiter behaviors.

Below are examples of where recruiters are already using AI in a useful way.

## Sourcing and market mapping

- turning rough hiring briefs into structured search angles
- researching adjacent companies and talent pools outside LinkedIn
- clustering prospects by likely fit, geography, or seniority

## Screening and candidate understanding

- summarizing resumes into consistent decision-ready notes
- comparing candidate profiles against role requirements
- turning scattered interview notes into sharper stakeholder updates

## Communication and follow-up

- drafting outreach variants for different candidate segments
- rewriting messages to match a more credible tone
- preparing personalized follow-ups faster without sounding robotic

## Internal recruiting operations

- drafting weekly pipeline updates
- turning hiring-manager notes into action items
- documenting repeatable search playbooks for future roles

## What strong recruiters still keep human

AI helps with speed, structure, and first-pass thinking. Human judgment still matters most for:

- deciding what good looks like
- choosing where nuance matters
- building trust with candidates and hiring managers
- knowing when a process needs redesign, not just acceleration
